Ransomware Hits a Law Firm — Client IDs, Case Files, and Confidential Legal Data Stolen and Threatened for Public Release

In January 2026, the INC Ransom group breached Hawk Law Group — a personal injury firm in Augusta, Georgia — stealing client government IDs, civil and criminal case files, and confidential legal data. The attackers publicly listed the firm and threatened to release everything unless payment was made. This is no longer a distant threat. It is targeting your industry.

Incident Overview

What Happened to Hawk Law Group

On January 31, 2026, the ransomware group INC Ransom — also known as Incransom — breached the systems of Hawk Law Group, a personal injury law firm based in Augusta, Georgia, with over 71 years of legal experience serving clients across the Central Savannah River Area. The breach was discovered and publicly listed on February 1st, when the attackers posted the firm on their dark web leak site and issued a direct threat: pay up, or the stolen data gets released to the public.

What was stolen makes this incident particularly alarming for any law firm: client government-issued IDs, civil and criminal case files, litigation-related forms, and what cybersecurity analysts described as potentially privileged client communications. As of late February 2026, Hawk Law Group had made no public statement acknowledging the breach — leaving clients with no official notification that their most sensitive legal and personal information may have been compromised.

This was not an isolated incident. In the same week, INC Ransom also listed two other law firms as victims on its leak site — Eisenberg Lowrance Lundell Lofgren and PK Pinhão & Koiffman Attorneys at Law — making clear that legal practices are being systematically targeted, not randomly selected.

Event Sequence

How the Attack Unfolded

January 31, 2026 — Breach
INC Ransom Infiltrates Hawk Law Group Systems
The ransomware group gained unauthorized access to the firm's systems, exfiltrating client data including government IDs, case files, and confidential legal documents. The firm did not publicly acknowledge the breach at this time.
February 1, 2026 — Public Listing
Firm Listed on Dark Web Leak Site — Ransom Demanded
INC Ransom posted Hawk Law Group on their public leak site, warning that the full data leak would be published unless a representative contacted them to negotiate. Two other law firms were listed the same week.
February 23, 2026 — No Response
Firm Still Listed — No Public Statement Issued
Nearly four weeks after the breach, Hawk Law Group had issued no public statement, filed no regulatory notices, and made no acknowledgment on its website. Clients had received no official notification. The firm remained listed on the attacker's leak site.

The full leak will be published soon, unless a company representative contacts us.

— INC Ransom Group, Dark Web Leak Site, February 1, 2026

Executive Takeaways

5 Things Every Law Firm Must Understand Right Now

  • 1
    Law firms are high-value targets — not incidental victims. Attackers go where the data is most sensitive and most valuable. Client IDs, case files, medical records, financial information, and privileged communications make law firms one of the most attractive targets in any sector.
  • 2
    Backups are no longer enough. INC Ransom uses double extortion — stealing data before deploying ransomware. Even if you restore your systems from backup, the stolen data can still be leaked publicly. The threat doesn't end when your systems come back online.
  • 3
    Silence after a breach creates its own crisis. Hawk Law Group made no public statement for weeks. In that vacuum, clients had no way to protect themselves from identity theft, fraud, or exposure of their legal strategy. Transparency and rapid client notification are both an ethical obligation and a trust-preservation strategy.
  • 4
    71 years of reputation can be undone overnight. Hawk Law Group has over seven decades of service to its community. A single breach — handled poorly — can erase that goodwill permanently. Cybersecurity is now as fundamental to a firm's reputation as its courtroom record.
  • 5
    Your clients' data is your responsibility — legally and ethically. Bar associations increasingly treat cybersecurity as a professional competence requirement. A breach of client confidential information is not just a technology failure — it is a potential ethical violation with regulatory consequences.

What This Means for Your Organization

Four Briefs. Four Sectors. One Unavoidable Conclusion.

Over the past four briefs we have covered a school district, a county government, a hospital system, and now a law firm. Different organizations. Different states. Different sectors. The same attackers. The same tactics. The same devastating results.

The INC Ransom group alone targeted three law firms in a single week. They are not waiting for firms to grow large enough to be worth targeting. They are targeting any firm that holds valuable data and lacks the defenses to stop them. A 71-year-old firm with deep community roots was not too established to be attacked. No firm is.
